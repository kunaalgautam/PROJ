What is MMA?

Mixed Martial Arts is a full-combat sport that incorporates striking, grappling, wrestling, and ground-fighting. It is different than boxing as it incorporates many other forms of combat. Over the past 20 years, the sport has evolved and become a worldwide sensation of an activity. Many MMA promotions have reigned above all in terms of popularity, however the main focus of this webpage will be of the two most current popular ones: the UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship) and Bellator. The sport itself can be very complicated to understand but for those who do not understand it, here is a brief explanation. Fighters are divided into separate weight classes to make the matchups between fighters more fair. In the UFC, there are multiple weight classes: flyweight (125 lb), bantamweight (135 lb), featherweight (145 lb), lightweight (155 lb), welterweight (170 lb), middleweight (185 lb), light heavyweight (205 lb), and heavyweight (with the largest weight limit discrepancy of anywhere between 206 lb to 265 lb). Fighters must make the weight limit during weigh ins which tend to be two days before fight day. If they are unable to make the proper weight requirement, then the fight could either be cancelled or the fighter may have to forfeit their purse. Bellator MMA does not have a flyweight division. Normally, fights are scheduled for 3 5-minute rounds. However the fight can be stopped anytime during the fight and rounds if a fighter is knocked out unconscious (considered a KO), if the fighter is repeatedly taking damage and unable to defend themselves (TKO), tap-out due to being choked or twisted (submission), or decision (all rounds end And the judges score the contest). Each weight class has their own respective champions and rankings which will be shown in this web page. Moreover, it is illegal to kick a grounded opponent anywhere above the waist. It is quite the spectacle!
